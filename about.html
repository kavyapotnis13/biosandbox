🧬 BioSandbox

About BioSandbox

BioSandbox is an interactive biology learning app for middle and high schoolers. Instead of reading static textbook diagrams, you explore — clicking organelles, stepping through DNA replication, matching base pairs, and testing yourself with quizzes.

Why

Biology is one of the most visual sciences, but most online resources are passive videos or dense text. BioSandbox closes that gap by letting you actually interact with the content — building real intuition for how cells, DNA, and proteins work.

How it's built

The whole app is a multi-page static website — just HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Interactive diagrams use SVG with click and hover handlers. Quizzes are JS objects pulled into multiple-choice questions. Progress saves to your browser's localStorage — no account needed.

For the Congressional App Challenge

BioSandbox is being built as a 2026 Congressional App Challenge submission. The curriculum maps to AP Bio and NGSS standards.
